# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Homeowners in West Houston (and surrounding affluent suburbs) looking to design, build, or maintain an outdoor space. They arrive from search or referral while researching landscaping and hardscape contractors, often comparing two or three firms. They're evaluating taste and trustworthiness as much as price, and the decision is high-consideration: a yard renovation is a meaningful spend. Context is typically desktop during research, mobile when first discovering or sharing with a partner.

## Product Purpose
A single-page marketing site for **2 Gals Landscaping**, a Houston landscaping & construction company. It exists to convert qualified local homeowners into quote requests. Success = a visitor submitting the "Get a Quote" form (or calling), having been convinced the firm does premium, lasting work. Secondary purpose: establish credibility (portfolio, team, process, testimonials) so the quote feels low-risk.

## Brand Personality
Premium and refined. Confident, polished, design-led, not chatty or folksy. The two-women-owned story is a point of distinction and warmth, but the surface should read as a high-end design-build firm that competes on taste, not as a neighborhood lawn-care service. Three words: refined, grounded, assured. Emotional goal: a visitor should feel "these people have taste, my yard is safe in their hands."

## Anti-references
- The generic contractor template: interchangeable green-gradient "lawn care" sites, stock clip-art, phone-number banners, badge soup. This is the explicit thing to avoid.
- Cold corporate / SaaS sterility (navy-and-gray, tech-startup chrome) would be the wrong register for outdoor craft.

## Design Principles
- **Let the work be the proof.** Real photography of finished landscapes carries credibility; copy supports, never substitutes.
- **Refined, not loud.** Premium reads through restraint, generous space, and committed but quiet color, not through more badges or brighter gradients.
- **Every section earns the quote.** Each scroll beat lowers perceived risk and moves toward the form; no section is decoration only.
- **Grounded confidence.** Assert capability plainly. No hype adjectives, no "transform your outdoor dreams" filler.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Light-on-dark hero text over video needs verified contrast and a solid poster fallback. Motion (parallax, marquee, scroll reveals) must fully respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Touch targets and tap-friendly nav for the mobile discovery path.
